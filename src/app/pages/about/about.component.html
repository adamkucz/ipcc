What is the IPCC?

The Inpatient Psychological Care Collaborative (IPCC) is an organization of health care professionals, academics, researchers, community members, and individuals with lived experience dedicated to promoting the development and dissemination of evidence-based psychological services in inpatient psychiatric settings. Our organization aims to improve psychological care in this setting by:
  • Maintaining an active member listserv for discussion and dissemination of inpatient psychological care
  • Advocating alongside patients, caregivers, researchers, and health care providers
  • Supporting programs of research focused on inpatient behavioral interventions
  • Providing pro-bono consultation on research methodology for individuals conducting research or quality-improvement projects aimed at improving inpatient psychological care
  • Compiling a list of instruments for the delivery of measurement-based care in the inpatient psychiatric setting

Steering Committee

Adam Kuczynski, PhD

Dr. Adam Kuczynski is a clinical psychologist and Acting Assistant Professor at the University of Washington School of Medicine, Department of Psychiatry and Behavioral Sciences. He received his PhD from the University of Washington in 2023 after completing his pre-doctoral internship at the same institution, training in serious mental illness and inpatient care at Harborview Medical Center and psycho-oncology Fred Hutch Cancer Center. Dr. Kuczynski currently works at the UW's long-term civil commitment program and studies strategies to improve inpatient care for individuals with serious mental illness.

Connor Adams, PsyD

Dr. Connor Adams is a psychologist and Clinical Assistant Professor who received her PsyD from the George Washington University and completed her internship training at Harvard Medical School/Cambridge Health Alliance. Her clinical and research interests center on therapeutic interventions that support recovery for individuals living with serious mental illness. Dr. Adams grounds her work in a psychodynamic perspective and has specialized training in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Psychosis (CBTp) and comprehensive Dialectical Behavior Therapy. She provides clinical services in both the inpatient and outpatient setting at Stanford Medical School.

Peter Phalen, PsyD

Dr. Peter Phalen is a clinical psychologist and Assistant Professor at the University of Maryland School of Medicine. His research focuses mostly on suicide, psychosis, and community mental health.

XIan Zhang, PsyD

Dr. Xian Zhang is a clinical psychologist with training in serious mental illness via inpatient care, psychiatric emergency rooms, partial hospitalization programs, and consult/liaison teams in public hospitals in Denver, Colorado and New York City, New York. She is passionate about improving trauma-informed inpatient care for marginalized populations who are navigating complex stressors and interpersonal dynamics, such as immigrant and BIPOC communities. She currently works in private practice in Seattle, Washington.

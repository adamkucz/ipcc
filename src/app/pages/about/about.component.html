Steering Committee

Adam Kuczynski, PhD

Dr. Adam Kuczynski is a clinical psychologist and Acting Assistant Professor at the University of Washington School of Medicine, Department of Psychiatry and Behavioral Sciences. He received his PhD from the University of Washington in 2023 after completing his pre-doctoral internship at the same institution, training in serious mental illness and inpatient care at Harborview Medical Center and psycho-oncology Fred Hutch Cancer Center. Dr. Kuczynski currently works at the UW's long-term civil commitment program and studies strategies to improve inpatient care for individuals with serious mental illness.

Alexandra Moussa-Tooks, PhD

Dr. Alexandra Moussa-Tooks is a clinical psychologist and Assistant Professor at Indiana University Bloomington (IUB). She received her dual PhD in Psychology and Neuroscience from IUB in 2021 after completing a pre-doctoral internship at Vanderbilt University Medical Center in the Early Psychosis Program. She studies sensorimotor processes and cerebellar system disturbances in psychotic disorder phenomenology. She is deeply committed to translational goals of her research, including applications for intervention, and understanding and rectifying racial bias in psychotic disorder assessment, diagnosis, and care, especially in the inpatient setting.

Connor Adams, PsyD

Dr. Connor Adams is a psychologist and Clinical Assistant Professor who received her PsyD from the George Washington University and completed her internship training at Harvard Medical School/Cambridge Health Alliance. Her clinical and research interests center on therapeutic interventions that support recovery for individuals living with serious mental illness. Dr. Adams grounds her work in a psychodynamic perspective and has specialized training in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Psychosis (CBTp) and comprehensive Dialectical Behavior Therapy. She provides clinical services in both the inpatient and outpatient setting at Stanford Medical School.

Awais Aftab, MD

Dr. Awais Aftab is a psychiatrist in Cleveland, OH, and a Clinical Assistant Professor of psychiatry at Case Western Reserve University. His academic, educational, and public-facing work focuses on conceptual and critical issues in the field. He has worked in the inpatient psychiatric setting with both civil and forensic patients. His first book, Conversations in Critical Psychiatry (2024), is an edited collection of interviews published by Oxford University Press. He is a senior editor for the journal “Philosophy, Psychiatry, & Psychology” and blogs online on his Substack newsletter Psychiatry at the Margins.

Peter Phalen, PsyD

Dr. Peter Phalen is an Assistant Professor and clinical psychologist at the University of Maryland School of Medicine. His research focuses mostly on psychosis, suicide, and community mental health. He is the Principal Investigator on a grant from NIMH for a clinical trial of DBT skills training for people with psychotic disorders at high risk of suicide. Dr. Phalen is an expert in applied statistical methods and has substantial experience implementing clinical programs across various settings, including in inpatient psychiatry units.

Xian Zhang, PsyD

Dr. Xian Zhang is a clinical psychologist with training in serious mental illness via inpatient care, psychiatric emergency rooms, partial hospitalization programs, and consult/liaison teams in public hospitals in Denver, Colorado and New York City, New York. She is passionate about improving trauma-informed inpatient care for marginalized populations who are navigating complex stressors and interpersonal dynamics, such as immigrant and BIPOC communities. She currently works in private practice in Seattle, Washington.

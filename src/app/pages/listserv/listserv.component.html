Listserv Guidelines

Welcome to our IPCC listserv! To ensure a productive and relational environment for all community members, please adhere to the following guidelines:

  1. Encourage Collaboration: Share ideas, resources, and feedback constructively, especially when it is beneficial for all members. When responding to others, aim to offer insight or build on their thoughts rather than simply critique.
  2. Foster Dialogue: Engage in discussions while being open to other opinions and perspectives. It can be helpful to specify when information is based on personal observations, patient experiences, or research literature.
  3. Be Respectful: Treat all members with kindness and respect. Please express differing opinions thoughtfully and without personal attacks.
  4. Stay On Topic: Please ensure your messages are relevant to IPCC's mission and values. If you have a topic that may be of interest to a specific individual but not the entire listserv, reach out to individuals privately. Similarly, contact individuals directly with products and services that you believe would help them, and avoid marketing products to the listserv.
  5. Respect Patient and Member Privacy: Comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and applicable state and federal privacy laws. Do not share PHI. Do not share members' contact information or private conversations without consent.
  6. Additional Etiquette to Consider: It is helpful to include your name and affiliation when posting. Specifying a topic in the subject line will encourage relevant responses. Send messages such as "thanks for posting" directly to the individual rather than the entire listserv. When you want to change or remove your listserv subscription, please follow the instructions on our website rather than emailing the listserv.
  7. Moderation and Conflict Resolution: If you see behavior that violates these guidelines, please report it to the list moderator by emailing (include contact information here). We aim to resolve conflicts amicably and maintain a supportive environment.
